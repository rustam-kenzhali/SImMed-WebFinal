Forearm model for intravenous injection




The simulator is a model made of durable plastic in the form of a relief tablet that conveys the configuration of the front surface of the forearm and hand. The model has niches for the installation of replaceable inserts. The inserts visually and palpationally imitate the skin, soft tissues and superficial veins, accurately convey tactile sensations when performing injections. All inserts are easily removable, and can be replaced as they wear out.
The simulator is designed to practice such skills as:

Intravenous injection of needles and catheters;
Infusion of solutions;
Blood collection from the veins of the elbow bend.


The simulator is designed to
- improve the effectiveness of training students, residents and practitioners in passing certification and accreditation.
